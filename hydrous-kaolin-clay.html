Hyrdrous Kaolin Clay
  • Properties
  • Technical Specification
  • Packing Sizes

The hydrothermal decomposition of granite rocks for over millions of years lead to the formation of hydrated Aluminum Silicate crystals. These crystals are commonly known as china clay or kaolin. These are fine sized particles, plate like shaped and they are chemically inert over wide range of pH. It has lower heat and electrical conductivity. The nature of kaolin differs from one mine to another. Thus it has to be processed depending upon its commercial use.
